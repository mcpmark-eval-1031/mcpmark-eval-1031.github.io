We introduce **In-Context Sharpness (ICS)**, a novel signal derived from LLM inner representations that measures the sharpness of in-context attention distributions. A drop in ICS correlates strongly with hallucination risk. We leverage ICS at inference time to guide decoding and substantially mitigate hallucinations across benchmarks.

**Authors:** Shiqi Chen, Miao Xiong, Junteng Liu, Zhengxuan Wu, Teng Xiao, Siyang Gao, Junxian He
